HOW WE DO IT

Services and a process that works

Discovery

Discoverying Potential

Discovery is a specialized type of career exploration provided for up to 90 days through referral from an individual’s Personal Agent or Service Coordinator at their Community Developmental Disabilities program. During Discovery we focus on “finding a person’s unique personal genius” as a method to help a person determine a career or job goal they will enjoy and be successful in. Discovery includes working with a team to complete an interactive vocational futures plan, visiting employers for tours and informational interviews on jobs. At the end of Discovery we will have an idea of a job direction, support needs, and a person’s skills, abilities, likes and successes.

Employment Path

The Path

Employment Path is a service to help an individual get ready for a job. Often this service allows ACES to provide a job coach for a work experience, job training, or volunteer work, complete a resume and portfolio, practice interviewing skills, etc. to get ready to begin job placement activities. This service also in contracted through an individual’s Personal Agent or Service Coordinator (at CSI, JCDD, or CLCM).

Job Development and Placement

Developing skills and placement

These services begin when an ACES participant, looking for work, is ready to find a job. It is a contracted service through our partnership with Vocational Rehabilitation Services and Oregon Commission for the Blind. It includes developing a plan for contacting employers, completing informational interviews to determine if a job is the right match, filling out job applications, help with interviews, and working with WorkSource Oregon on finding job openings or training opportunities.

Job Coaching

This service can be provided during Employment Path or when an individual is in a work experience or volunteer work, but usually begins when a participant is hired into a paid individual community job. ACES Job Coaches are trained professionals, required to obtain national certification in this field. They are available to provide training and support for a participant on the job. Initial job coaching for participants is provided through contract with Vocational Rehabilitation Services and Oregon Commission for the Blind. Ongoing coaching supports then can be provided through the Community Developmental Disabilities Program/brokerage partnership.

Supported Self-Employment

Exploration of Options

A valid vocational goal can be self-employment. ACES can provide help to a participant wishing to start a business, including step-by-step help in the marketing and financial feasibility determinations, research of the business idea, completion of a business plan, and job coaching support to begin a business.

High School to Work Transition

If you are a high school student, we help you research career possibilities and colleges, locate financial aid, and complete college entrance and financial aid applications. If you have a disability, we help you research college disability services to obtain the accommodations you need to succeed.

Social Security Benefits Planning

ACES works with local resource agencies, such as WIN, to help participants understand how working will affect their social security benefits. Also, many social security recipients can access vocational training funding and supports through Social Security. We help participants identify and access these resources when they are available, to support their competitive job goal or self-employment.
